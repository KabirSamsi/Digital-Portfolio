Two Point Perspective Sketch




Mediums: 0.7 mm Mechanical Pencil, Sketchbook Paper


After learning about 1-point perspective, we began to look at 2-point perspective. Like 1-point perspective, it incorporated a horizon line, but contained 2, instead of 1 vanishing points, thus the name. This gave it views from the front and the two sides, and unlike 1-point perspective, which was all about shrinking away, 2-point perspective gave more views. As shown in the image above, this gave the image a box-like view. In our first sketch, we compared how images looked in 2-point perspective if drawn below, above or on the horizon line. It was a fun activity and later contributed to our final half-cycle assignment.
